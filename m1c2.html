Why Is Chewing Gum a Hidden Environmental Threat?

This is an Interactive Video, so explore it and listen carefully.

Environmental Threat

Conventional chewing gums, made with synthetic polymers like polyvinyl acetate, contribute to ~100,000,000 kg of plastic waste annually. As the second most common litter, gum sticks to surfaces, pollutes water as microplastics, and persists in ecosystems, harming the planet.

Watch the Problem Explained

Key Insight

Plastic-based gums create non-degradable waste, with microplastics in food chains posing health risks.

Understanding the Impact

Chewing gum contributes to ~100,000,000 kg of plastic waste yearly.
Non-biodegradable gum litters streets and waterways.
Microplastics from gum enter food chains, affecting wildlife and humans.
Sustainable alternatives like plant-based gum reduce environmental harm.
