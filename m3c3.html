Business Model

How does GudGum make money and get its eco-friendly gum to you?

Business Model Overview

How does GudGum make money and get its eco-friendly gum to you? Through a smart mix of online sales, retail partnerships, and event appearances, GudGum reaches conscious chewers while staying true to its sustainable roots. Let’s break down the business model!

Direct-to-Consumer (D2C)

GudGum is sold directly through www.gudgum.in, Amazon, Flipkart, and Blinkit (in Bengaluru). Enjoy free shipping on orders over ₹360, making it easy to grab your favorite eco-conscious gum online.

Retail

Find GudGum in eco-stores like Brown Living™, where sustainable shoppers can pick up our gum in person. These stores align with our green mission and bring GudGum to local communities.

Events

GudGum shines at vegan markets and sustainability events, connecting with eco-conscious crowds. Our premium pricing (₹350–₹1,584) reflects high-quality, natural ingredients, though 74% of consumers note the price premium for sustainable products

Revenue Figures

  • FY 2022–23: ₹29.7 Lakhs
  • FY 2023–24 (till November): ₹53 Lakhs

In-House Manufacturing

GudGum manufactures in-house, ensuring strict quality control and minimal production waste. This hands-on approach reflects our commitment to sustainable practices and top-notch gum. Watch how GudGum comes to life, one chew at a time!

Digital-First Marketing

To build brand awareness, GudGum goes big on digital marketing! Our strategies include:

  • Engaging social media campaigns to showcase our eco-friendly vibe.
  • Influencer collaborations with eco-conscious content creators.
  • Educational promotions highlighting the benefits of natural, plastic-free products.

This approach builds a community of conscious consumers who love sustainable, mindful living.

Key Selling Channels

GudGum reaches you through multiple channels:

  • Official Website: Order directly from www.gudgum.in.
  • Online Marketplaces: Available on Amazon and Flipkart.
  • Retail Stores: Found in eco-stores and health shops in select cities.

Sales are evenly split: 50% online marketplaces (Amazon, Flipkart) and 50% offline retail.

Key Takeaways

  • GudGum’s revenue comes from a balanced mix of D2C (online) and retail (offline) channels, with ₹53 Lakhs earned by November 2024.
  • In-house manufacturing ensures quality and minimal waste, aligning with sustainable practices.
  • Digital-first marketing (social media, influencers, education) builds a community of eco-conscious consumers.
